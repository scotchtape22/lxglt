Intro

My name is Lance Xyler! The greatest Lazer Tag gunner in the history of the sport! In this internet simulation, you will manage a team to the heights of Lazer Tag glory against your friends.

Lazer Tag

In a game of laser tag, 2 teams field 8 players each. For 4 quarters of 8 rounds each, these players will score points by hitting shooting each other (1 point) or attacking each other's core tower (worth 4 points). The team with the most points at the end of the game is victorious.

Your Roster

Each team will have up to 36 players on their roster.

  • 8 Starting Players - Your go to players on most plays
  • 16 Substitute Players - Can be swapped in for special tactics or in case of injury
  • 4 Scratched Players - Players on the roster who are currently injured, in rehab, or just not needed for play
  • 8 Minor League Players - Players playing in the minor league to develop skills

Your Players

Each player has several different stats.

  • Accuracy- A player's shooting ability
  • Evasion- A player's dodging ability
  • Initative- How quick a player acts each round
  • Physique- A player's ability to avoid injury
  • Potential- A player's ability to learn more and level up
  • Knowledge- A player's general knowledge of Lazer Tag and ability to share it with others
  • Ego- A player's knowledge of how good they are, Lance Xyler knows this well.

Players stats can be amplified, which increases their variability nullified which decreases their variability, or modified which changes their value. Along with this, player's will have a hometown, age, injuries, and relationships, some of these might come into play, some of them might now. That's not for Lance Xyler to say.

No player plays for free, you will have to sign each player to a contract.

  • Game Salary- How much this player is paid per game.
  • Bonuses- Bonueses for reachinng certain milestones either as a team or as an individual player.
  • Contract Term- How long the contract lasts for.
  • Contract Type- What type of contract that this is:
    • Assistant Coach- Player serves as a specific type of coach, see "Office" for more details.
    • Franchise- Player doesn't count towards your salary cap, cannot be traded, must be a starter, limit 3.
    • Standard- Player is a regular part of the team, and must always be on the professional team, limit 28.
    • 2-Way- Player can be a part of the profession or minor league team, limit 8.
    • Minor League- Player can only be part of the minor league team, does not count towards salary cap, limit 8

Along with this, player's will have a hometown, age, injuries, and relationships, some of these might come into play, some of them might not. That's not for Lance Xyler to say.

Your Game Plan

Before each game, you will come up with plans for how to perform in the line of fire, there are 3 major parts to this. First are your strategies.

Strategy

You can set up between 1 and 5 strategies, each tactic has a preference that will help you determine how often it is used. For each strategy, your 8 starters will have a choice of tactic:

  • Charge- Amplify your initative, nullify your accuracy.
  • Camp- Amplify your evasion, nullify your initiative.
  • Assault- Amplify your accuracy, nullify your evasion.
  • Free Fire- Take an extra shots. Slightly decrease all stats.
  • Flank- Attempt to rush your opponents control tower.
  • Ambush- Attack player's attempting to rush your tower.

Depth Chart

Players are set to different parts of the roster (bench, starter, scratch, minor leagues). For benched players on each strategy, they choose a player to sub for in case this starter must leave the game early.

Your Office and Budget

Along with the roster, you will have to manage the budget of your team and its staff.

Staff

Player's can be hired into assistant coaching roles, these usue knowledge to assist your team as a whole.

  • Mentors- Help players level up, limit 2 (one for the pro team, one for the minor team).
  • Medics- Help players heal injuries and addictions, limit 1.
  • Marketeer- Helps you earn more cash, limit 1.
  • Scouts- Used to scout players on other teams and minor league players, limit 3.

Stadium and Fan Experience

Your stadium and fan experience will affect your income. Stadium Capacity affects the maximum amount of money that can be collected during a match, fan experience affects the minimum amount of income.

Budget

Each game is going to incure some operating costs and earn you profit.

Suppliers

Player's can be hired into assistant coaching roles, these usue knowledge to assist your team as a whole.

  • Mentors- Help players level up, limit 2 (one for the pro team, one for the minor team).
  • Medics- Help players heal injuries and addictions, limit 1.
  • Marketeer- Helps you earn more cash, limit 1.
  • Scouts- Used to scout players on other teams and minor league players, limit 3.

Running a League

Leagues will have a number of settings, including:

  • Number of teams (4-24)
  • Number of games (short, medium, long) seasons
  • Number of playoff teams
  • Salary cap
